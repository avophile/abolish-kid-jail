Understanding Manifestation Determination Hearings

Federal law protects students with disabilities from discriminatory discipline. A manifestation determination hearing is your child's legal safeguard against being punished for behavior related to their disability.

What is a Manifestation Determination Hearing?

A manifestation determination hearing is a legally required meeting that must occur when a student with an IEP (Individualized Education Program) faces disciplinary action that results in removal from school for more than 10 days.

This protection is part of the Individuals with Disabilities Education Act (IDEA), a federal law that ensures students with disabilities receive appropriate education and are not discriminated against due to their disability.

When is a Manifestation Determination Required?

A hearing is required when:

  • Your child is suspended for more than 10 consecutive school days
  • Your child has multiple suspensions that total more than 10 days and form a pattern
  • The school is recommending expulsion
  • Your child is placed in an interim alternative educational setting for up to 45 days
  • Any disciplinary action that constitutes a "change in placement"

The Two Critical Questions

The manifestation determination team (which includes you, teachers, and school administrators) must answer these two questions:

Question 1: Was the conduct caused by, or directly and substantially related to, the child's disability?

What this means: Did your child's disability cause or contribute to the behavior?

Examples:

  • A student with ADHD acted impulsively (impulsivity is a symptom of ADHD)
  • A student with autism had a meltdown when routines changed (difficulty with transitions is autism-related)
  • A student with an emotional disturbance had an outburst when overwhelmed
  • A student with intellectual disability didn't understand social boundaries

Question 2: Was the conduct a direct result of the school's failure to implement the IEP?

What this means: Was the school actually following your child's IEP? If they had been, would this have happened?

Examples:

  • The IEP requires a behavior plan, but the school wasn't following it
  • The IEP specifies accommodations (breaks, small group), but teachers weren't providing them
  • The IEP requires check-ins with a counselor, but they weren't happening
  • The IEP has supports that would have prevented this, but they weren't in place

If the answer to EITHER question is "yes," the behavior is a manifestation of the disability.

What Happens if It IS a Manifestation?

If the team determines the behavior was a manifestation of the disability:

  • The school CANNOT proceed with typical discipline (suspension, expulsion)
  • Your child generally returns to their previous placement
  • The school must conduct a Functional Behavioral Assessment (FBA) if one hasn't been done or isn't working
  • The school must develop or revise a Behavior Intervention Plan (BIP)
  • An IEP meeting should be scheduled to address the behavior and add necessary supports

This protects your child from being punished for disability-related behavior and ensures they get support instead.

What Happens if It is NOT a Manifestation?

If the team determines the behavior was NOT a manifestation:

  • The school CAN proceed with standard disciplinary procedures
  • However, your child must still receive educational services (FAPE - Free Appropriate Public Education)
  • Services must continue even in an alternative setting
  • Your child must receive services to address the behavior (FBA and BIP services)
  • You have the right to appeal through an expedited due process hearing

Why This Matters: The School-to-Prison Pipeline

Students with disabilities are disproportionately suspended and expelled from school. When children with disabilities are pushed out of school through inappropriate discipline:

  • They lose critical educational opportunities
  • They're more likely to get involved with law enforcement
  • They're at higher risk of entering the juvenile justice system
  • Their long-term outcomes suffer significantly

Manifestation determination hearings exist to stop this pipeline. They ensure that children aren't criminalized for behaviors related to their disabilities, and that schools provide support rather than punishment.

Your Rights as a Parent

Under IDEA, you have the right to:

  • Demand a manifestation determination hearing when required
  • Participate as an equal member of the manifestation determination team
  • Review all relevant records before and during the meeting
  • Bring an advocate or attorney to the meeting
  • Present information and documentation supporting your position
  • Disagree with the decision and appeal through due process
  • Have your child remain in placement ("stay-put") during appeals
  • Receive written notice of the determination and the reasons for it
